Revamping the Momentum 947 Cycle Challenge website

947 Cycle Challenge is the world's second-largest timed cycle race, after the Cape Argus Cycle Race. Usually held annually on the third Sunday of November in Johannesburg.

Digital design Momentum

Design phase

I was tasked me with redesigning the Momentum 947 Cycle Challenge website due to the company's dissatisfaction with its current state. They sought a revitalized appearance. This project marked my debut into significant assignments at PRIMEDIA Broadcasting.

I collaborated closely with a dedicated back-end developer, supported by additional team members. Employing an agile and iterative methodology, I facilitated design sprints and maintained ongoing communication with the developers. This ensured the feasibility of our ideas within the project's timeframe.

Landing page

Kiddies Ride and Mountain Bike Challenge

The 947 Mountain Bike Challenge precedes the road race weekend. It offers participants two distance options: a 30 km (19 mi) race and a 55 km (34 mi) race. Additionally, in 2012, a 40 km (25 mi) fun ride was introduced, taking place the day before the 94.7 Mountain Bike Challenge.

Event Calendar

The redesign added many new sections, marking my first big digital project. One highlight was creating an interactive event calendar, blending user needs with technical know-how to make it seamless.

The calendar posed significant technical challenges. I navigated the complexities of UX and information architecture, prioritizing user tasks throughout. Our design aimed to empower visitors, enabling them to effortlessly create, edit, and view both new and existing events.

Training section

The Training Section marked the project's next exhilarating chapter. Like every other facet of the site, it brimmed with rich content, adding another layer of excitement to the journey.

It aimed to aid riders in race preparation, offering valuable insights on nutrition, training tips, and a wealth of other resources.

We organized the content to accommodate various types of riders, including:

  • Novice,
  • Intermediate,
  • and Advanced riders.

The take away

The Momentum 947 Cycle Challenge website redesign was my first attempt at User Experience design. I was thrown into the deep-end and had to quickly learn to swim with the sharks.

Due to the nature of the project I was constantly learning new ways of doing things, approaching things differently. Working with the Dev team meant that we had to be agile and applied an iterative approach, I also had to learn to use new tools like MVC (Microsoft Visual Studio) IDE in order to accomodate the dot net developer I worked with. At that time I got of UX literature from UXPin through their eBooks and downloads.
