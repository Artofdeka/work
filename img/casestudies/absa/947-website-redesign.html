A complete overhaul: 947 website redesign

947 is a radio station that broadcasts from Johannesburg, South Africa. If Joburg is South Africa’s heart, then 947 is its racing pulse, keeping listeners connected to the city, engaged with the boldest personalities, and moving to the biggest hits.

UX/UI design Web design 947
My role

User Experience
Visual Design
Prototyping

Date

2019, (6 months)

Client

Primedia Broadcasting, 947

High level goals
  1. Give listeners more control over their time.
  2. Create a platform that inspires deeper engagement.
  3. Reducing clutter and improve user satisfaction.

The challenge

In 2014, 947 launched a brand-new website. Fast-forward a few years later, the site looks outdated, unpolished, and riddled with poor usability. An overhaul was overdue. It didn't require a site audit for anyone to notice the site was breaking. Inconsistencies in the UI caused a lot of frustrations and drop offs, so we set out to create a principle-based design that puts users first.

Some of the pain points:
  • Podcasts not easily discoverable
  • Website not mobile friendly
  • Difficulty in finding shows/lineup times
  • Pages are content heavy

The solution

Discovery and ideation

We came up with a human-centred approach to design that focused on usability, utility and desirability. This required a new design system and a principled approach to our methodology.

Based on the insights from our research, it was clear that there was a gap between what listeners expected compared to what the site gave them.

Our goal was to reposiion the 947 website as a hub where listeners would be updated on the latest in music, lifestyle and current affairs and most importantly – the best of Joburg.

Reframing the problem

947 Listeners need a hub to keep them connected to all things Joburg, engaged with the boldest personalities and the biggest hits.

Design Phase

Tesing & validation

In aligning the designs to listeners’ requirements and expectation, We produced Task Analysis and User Journey maps to define a logical structure and flow.

The app design included the comprehensive and diverse functionality which had to be presented to users in a simple and clear way. The designers had to analyse and prfioritize all the points, as there was a high risk of overloading the screen. By research and testing, the user scenarios were created to determine which information about the meal in the recipe is found the most important.

Visual thinking on paper

I drew a few sketches to bring ideas to life and evaluated them with users. This helped me work rapidly and iteratively, and I generated stacks of ideas about UI arrangement, building bloack and components, and finally page flows.

1st Iteration

I created the initial high-fidelity prototypes and tested it with users which highlighted few problem areas, then went back to iterate.

Streamlining the navigation

While testing we noticed that some listeners were looking for a back button to navigate to the previous screen… To circumvent this we added back buttons and breadcrumbs which helped listeners better track their whereabouts and made things more intuitive.

Adding CTA’s on banners

Further testing revealed that some users ignored the featured content banners on the homepage while others aren’t sure what to do. We made the necessary iterations which allowed users to know what type of content they were looking at and take the necessary steps, it also allowed them to know what to expect when clicking on the banner.
